## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Not AI slop.** The site inverts nearly every cliché: no cream background (a computed, living sky instead), no eyebrow kickers, no card grids, no gradient text, no hero metrics. Lowercase voice, wavy underlines, polaroid lightbox, and a sky engine computing real star positions read as one person's taste executed with rigor. Residual risk is the opposite failure: the homepage is so spare it can read as under-informative.

**Deterministic scan:** 1 CLI finding (`broken-image` on Lightbox.astro:31's empty `img src`) — false positive; the img lives in a closed dialog and is populated on open. Browser overlay: home 3 / about 5 hits. Both `low-contrast` hits (1.0–1.1:1) are measurement artifacts — the detector fell back to white when it found no solid ancestor background, because the backdrop is the sky gradient (the site's own `pickInk` system verifies real contrast). `ai-color-palette` on `#horizon` is the deliberate dusk simulation, not slop. Genuine hits: `tight-leading` (line-height 1.25 on 11px gallery hover captions) and `image-hover-transform` (hover scale on gallery imgs — a deliberate, conventional choice).

## Overall Impression

A personal site where the gimmick is load-bearing: the sky isn't decoration, it's the work sample. Craft depth (contrast-by-construction, lightbox focus choreography) is senior-level and mostly invisible — which is also the problem. The biggest opportunity: the site never tells anyone what it's doing (the real sky) or who Amy is professionally (no role line, no OG meta). The peak experience is fragile because it's unsignposted.

## What's Working

1. **AAA by construction, not by audit** — `pickInk`/`chipFor`/`scrimFor` in sky.ts verify contrast against the live gradient band and synthesize a compliant chip/scrim when no ink passes. Rare, senior craft; held in every observed state.
2. **The gimmick is load-bearing** — real star catalog with sidereal-time placement, moon phase, geolocated sun. An argument for the "design engineering" positioning, not a parallax toy.
3. **Lightbox focus choreography** — focus parked on the polaroid, restored across media swaps, returned to the originating tile on close; edge-pinned arrows matching Google Photos muscle memory.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] Fixed chips occlude bio text on mobile.** At 375px the "▶ 24h"/"sky: auto" pills and horizon band overlay `.sky-card` prose mid-scroll. The site's thesis is "text is always readable"; the chips break it mechanically instead of chromatically. Fix: bottom padding on `main` ≥ horizon-band height, or fade chips while scrolling. → /impeccable adapt
- **[P1] No identity payload for the 8-second visitor or link preview.** Homepage is name + icons (no role line); Layout.astro ships no `og:*`/`twitter:card` meta — a shared link renders bare. Recruiters arrive via shared links and skim. Fix: one lowercase subtitle under the h1 + OG meta with a dusk-sky image. → /impeccable clarify
- **[P2] Lightbox navigation is silent to assistive tech.** Arrows/swipe swap img/caption with no announcement; focus stays on a non-announcing div. Fix: SR-only `aria-live="polite"` status ("photo 4 of 9 — {alt}"). → /impeccable harden
- **[P2] Carousel nav gated by width, not pointer.** `max-width: 640px` hides prev/next but swipe is touch-only — a mouse user in a narrow window has only undiscoverable arrow keys. Fix: hide on `(pointer: coarse) and (max-width: 640px)` instead. Also verify Escape-to-close on a real browser. → /impeccable harden
- **[P3] 24h demo is uninterruptible and unindicated.** No running state, no stop for ~14s. Fix: "◼ stop" label while running + early exit. → /impeccable polish

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (first-timer):** h1 + three icons answer *who* but never *what* — 5-second test fails for a job-search site. "▶ 24h" is an unlabeled mystery box; "dusk · san francisco" is actively confusing when Jordan is in Denver and geo fell back. The only door deeper ("about") is a tiny corner link.

**Casey (one-handed mobile):** chip cluster covers the "ACL coper" paragraph mid-scroll; hover-only grid captions mean tiles are context-free until opened; lightbox Close is out of thumb range (backdrop-tap rescues but is undiscoverable).

**Sam (screen reader/keyboard):** silent photo swaps in the lightbox; "Play video" autoplays with sound, no captions track; at narrow widths keyboard carousel nav exists but has zero visible affordance. Otherwise strong: full alt text, labeled dialog, visible focus rings, focus restoration.

## Minor Observations

## Questions to Consider

1. The sky engine is the portfolio's real work sample — is the unexplained mystery worth more than a whispered caption ("your sky · 9:41pm · denver")?
2. If "text is sacred" is the brand, shouldn't occlusion be treated as seriously as contrast?
3. The homepage bets everything on restraint. For a Bay Area job search in 2026, is that confidence or cost — would one lowercase line under the name break the spell or complete it?
